The Methodology

A 6-phase diagnostic that
transforms data into strategy

SMEs already have the data. The delay in traditional consulting comes from organizational process, not analysis. With direct data access, diagnostics can be executed in days — not months.

Phase 01

Business Context & Data Access

A 60–90 minute leadership interview covering business model, revenue structure, pricing, sales process, growth challenges, and current KPIs. We map all available data sources and secure system access to define the full audit scope.

Typical Systems Accessed
  • CRM (HubSpot, Zoho, Salesforce)
  • Google Ads / Meta Ads
  • Accounting (Tally, Zoho Books, QuickBooks)
  • ERP, dashboards, spreadsheets
Phase 02

Data Consolidation

We extract, normalize, and unify data from all identified systems into a single analytical environment using Python, SQL, and automated data pipelines. Clean, structured datasets are built for each business dimension.

Datasets Prepared
  • Revenue and sales funnel data
  • Marketing spend and attribution
  • Customer acquisition and retention
  • Cost structures and margins
Phase 03

Core Performance Diagnostics

Deep quantitative analysis across five dimensions using funnel analysis, cohort analysis, CAC/LTV modeling, regression diagnostics, and segmentation. Every metric is benchmarked against industry standards to identify performance gaps.

Analytical Techniques
  • Funnel & cohort analysis
  • CAC / LTV modeling
  • Unit economics modeling
  • Regression & segmentation analysis
Phase 04

Revenue Leak Detection

We identify precisely where revenue is being lost — funnel conversion gaps, CAC inefficiency, pricing below contribution margins, retention failures, and pipeline stalls. Each leak is financially quantified using revenue driver modeling.

How Impact Is Estimated
  • Conversion rate vs benchmark delta
  • CAC vs LTV ratio analysis
  • Margin compression modeling
  • Churn impact on annual revenue
Phase 05

Opportunity Identification

Beyond fixing problems, we uncover measurable growth levers: conversion optimization, marketing budget reallocation, pricing adjustments, cross-sell and upsell potential, automation opportunities, and high-value segment focus.

Opportunity Types
  • Revenue growth levers
  • Cost efficiency improvements
  • Automation & process optimization
  • High-margin segment prioritization
Phase 06

Fix / Improve / Ignore Prioritization

Every finding is classified into one of three categories with a financial impact estimate and clear recommended action. Leadership receives an executive presentation with a 30-day implementation priority list. No ambiguity. No jargon.

Final Deliverables
  • 20–25 slide executive report
  • Financially quantified revenue leaks
  • Fix / Improve / Ignore roadmap
  • Live leadership presentation + Q&A

What We Typically Find

The revenue leaks hiding
in most SMEs

These patterns appear in the majority of audits. Most leadership teams are unaware they exist — because the data proving it sits in disconnected systems.

Leak 01 📉

Sales Funnel Conversion Collapse

The most common revenue leak. Large pipeline volume with poor lead qualification, causing low close rates and wasted sales effort. The drop occurs between lead generation and qualified opportunity — and is invisible without CRM-to-marketing data cross-referencing.

Most frequent finding
Leak 02 📣

Scaling Unprofitable Channels

Running campaigns without true CAC vs LTV visibility. Companies scale marketing spend into channels generating customers below contribution margin — often discovered only when overall margins start declining quarter after quarter.

Most expensive mistake
Leak 03 ⚙️

Manual Process Bottlenecks

Manual processes slowing fulfillment or delivery cycles, silently limiting growth capacity. These operational inefficiencies don't appear in revenue reports — but they reduce repeat purchase rates, damage customer satisfaction, and cap the business's ability to scale.

Silent growth limiter
Leak 04 💰

Margin Compression from Pricing Gaps

Pricing set by market assumption rather than unit economics and contribution margin analysis. As costs scale, margin compresses silently. Most businesses discover this only when they're growing revenue but losing profitability simultaneously.

Found in 70% of audits
Leak 05 👥

Misallocated Customer Acquisition

Most revenue comes from a small high-value segment — but acquisition spend is distributed equally across all customer types. Companies discover they've been investing heavily in acquiring customers who never reach LTV breakeven.

High-impact opportunity
Leak 06 🔁

Preventable Customer Churn

Churn patterns that are systematic and preventable — but only visible through cohort analysis. Companies treat all churn as random attrition when in fact most exits follow predictable behavioral patterns that can be identified and interrupted before they happen.

LTV impact: significant

Common Questions

Everything you
need to know

Answers to the questions leadership teams ask before requesting an audit.

What data do we need to provide?+
We work with your existing data — whatever systems you already use. Typically CRM (HubSpot, Zoho, Salesforce), Google Ads and Meta Ads, Google Analytics, accounting software (Tally, Zoho Books, QuickBooks), ERP systems, and internal spreadsheets. No special infrastructure required. If it exists in your business, we can work with it.
What if our data is messy or incomplete?+
Most businesses we work with have imperfect data — that's the norm, not the exception. We handle normalization, deduplication, and gap identification as part of Phase 2. Data quality limitations are documented clearly, and analysis focuses on what the available data can reliably tell us.
How is Monarch different from hiring a consultant?+
Traditional consulting engagements take 2–4 months and rely on interviews and frameworks rather than your actual data. Monarch is data-first — every finding is backed by your actual business numbers, every recommendation is financially quantified, and the process takes days, not months. We explain why metrics behave the way they do and what to change.
How is it different from a BI tool or dashboard?+
Dashboards show what your metrics are. Monarch explains why they behave the way they do and gives you a prioritized decision roadmap. BI tools require internal analytical expertise to interpret. Monarch delivers interpretation, financial impact estimates, and clear recommended actions — without requiring you to build internal capability first.
What if nothing major is found?+
The audit still produces performance benchmarks, optimization opportunities, and confirmation that the business is operating efficiently. Leadership gains clarity about where focus should be placed and which metrics actually drive outcomes — which is itself a valuable strategic output. This outcome is rare, but if it happens, you'll know your fundamentals are sound.
What happens after the audit?+
The audit is fully standalone — you receive a complete report and roadmap with no obligation to continue. Many clients move to the monthly retainer to maintain continuous analytical support, track implementation outcomes, and receive ongoing performance optimization. But the decision is entirely yours.
How is confidentiality handled?+
All engagements operate under a strict NDA signed before any data is shared. Data is stored in secured analytical environments and removed after project completion unless the retainer continues. We never discuss client specifics, business data, or findings with any third party under any circumstances.
What is the first client call like?+
A 60–90 minute leadership interview covering your business model, revenue structure, growth challenges, current data systems, and KPIs. It's a focused discovery conversation — not a sales pitch. By the end, we'll know whether we can help you and what the audit scope will cover.
